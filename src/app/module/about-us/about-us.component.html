Accreditation with International Yoga Alliance


International Yoga Alliance is a charity-based Yoga association with its headquarters in the USA. The work of the Alliance is carried out by those who have dedicated their lives to yoga and its purity, and not as a full-time business occupation or to gain name and fame, but, like Saint Patanjali, whose actual name is not known to anyone, these volunteers remain anonymous. IYA is a union of world Yoga enthusiasts that is intended for true promoters of Yoga, aimed at bringing all yoga communities and individuals across the world under one umbrella ‘Vasudaika Kutumbham’ / world as one family.

IYA is the authentic organisation with strong roots in the Himalayas and associations with traditional ashrams and gompas, and their heads. These are the places where those great yogis from Swamy Vivekananda to Tirumalai Krishnamacharya got their education and where great figures like Annie Besant and Rudolf Steiner experienced the reality beyond their imagination and went on to become creators of traditions, Steiner establishing Waldorf education/Steiner Schools with Emil Molt. The Himalayan traditions are the origins of all the healing teachings and the place where these nnovators gained their knowledge.

IYA is committed to represent traditions with an open mind that does not levy any subscription fee from its members. It allows its registered members to promote and teach yoga as certified, indicating that they have the base and background in different formats and in a wide spectrum of yoga, as preserved for thousands of years, and which has flowed from generation to generation up to the modern contemporary traditions.

The Alliance’s aim is not the promotion of yoga in the name of associations which, directly or indirectly collect money in the name of promotion of yoga. That is far from the tradition which has existed for thousands of years promoting yoga as a selfless activity. IYA is neither a business nor a livelihood entity for anyone promoting yoga, it is for the purpose of serving the yoga community with soul in its heart.

IYA is an organisation which, unlike other associations, issues authentic certifications to schools, teachers, studios and ashrams the world over, to whomsoever is committed and has proven that they are practicing or teaching yoga or promoting yoga in the right spirit. We are proud to be associated with these teachers, schools, studios and ashrams, and we issue certificates upon registration in turn

Yoga Healing Foundation


The Yoga Healing Foundation is a registered charity that runs Traditional Yoga courses for the benefit of the general public and the yoga community. This includes meditation courses, weekly classes, workshops, meditation teacher training courses, lectures and an annual ten day summer meditation retreat. Our courses are all run as a charitable not-for profit activity and cover theory and practice. Our purpose is to protect, revive and disseminate original and complete yogic knowledge by staying connected to the values and lineage of the Yoga tradition.

The Yoga Healing Foundation runs on the basis of two core values, Seva and Dana.

Seva, translated as selfless service, is an important principle in the yoga tradition. It is based on the value of working for others without expectation of personal reward. Karma yoga - path of dedication. This approach helps us to develop the qualities of acceptance, gratitude, helpfulness and generosity. Dana is the virtue of unconditional generosity and unattached giving through donation to a good cause. Traditionally, the teachings of yoga have for thousands of years been offered on the basis of Dana.

As much as we are able to follow this principle, our courses are given for free or are charged at a comparatively low cost. The unconditional donations received allows us to cover our basic expenses, and we welcome donations in gratitude for the teaching and in order to further our charitable work.

The act of giving a donation rather than a compulsory fee is an opportunity for participants in our courses to benefit from an act of voluntary giving and develop the yogic quality of generosity. Dana is one of the ten ‘perfected virtues’ taught by Buddha

Dr A L V Kumar

“Yoga is many limbed and is not to be confused with any form of religion. My aim is to restore the understanding of the relationship of different branches of traditional yoga so as to enable each person to make swift and effective progress”

Dr A L V Kumar

Dr. Kumar is widely recognized as an inspirational Yogi, trainer of yoga teachers and an award-winning scientist. He began his yoga journey at the age of 11 years and attended more than 87 traditional ashrams, learning directly under the expert guidance of great teachers such as Satyananda Saraswathi, Krishnamacharya and all the seven students of Sivananda, as well as in many Himalayan traditions.

Dr. Kumar is a nuclear scientist and holds three PhDs in the fields of Chemistry and Yoga. He is also a trained Merck pharmaceuticals and nutraceuticals formulation specialist. His teachings are not only limited to yoga and meditation, as he also has authority in science-based functional and performance-based nutraceuticals both macro and micro nutrition. Dr. Kumar practices preventive and therapeutic nutrition spanning from phytoextracts to nutrigenomics and peptide-based solutions for gene related problems. He is the founder of Vedic Neutraceuticals and The Yoga Healing Foundations of UK, U.S and India. He is a recipient of the prestigious Homi J. Bhabha award for innovations in nuclear science and technology. Dr. Kumar’s personal experience of the therapeutic benefits of yoga inspired him to teach. In 1992, Dr. Kumar suffered multiple fractures in his lower spine, hip bones, and pelvic bones, lost control over his lower torso and ruptured his urethra, when he had a road accident. Having just got engaged, he was told that he would not be able to have children and may not walk again. He was airlifted to his wedding.

After being bed-ridden for over a year and being told that doctors had little hope for his recovery, finally Dr. Kumar decided to start treating himself. He used his deep knowledge of yoga to design a systematic program of treatment for himself with asana and pranayama practice. By the following year, he was mobile and in two years he could run. In 1994 Dr. Kumar and his wife were blessed with their first child.

This was a turning point for him, and he decided to devote his time besides his work to promote the correct understanding of the traditional methods of yoga for body and mind. This includes all physical yoga practices as taught by well-known teachers from whom he learned directly, including Krishnamacharya, Vishnudevananda, Satyananda Saraswati, B.K.S. Iyengar and Pattabhi Jois.

Dr. Kumar’s teachings also include mind-fitness (meditation) programs in Hindu, Tibetan and Theravada traditions, taught at a deep level with a science-based approach for rapid progress. His teachings include progressive practice starting with mantra, through to the highest-level mindfulness culminating in self-realisation, actualisation or Singularity.

In essence, Dr. Kumar teaches a scientific way of life for total health. He shows us how to live with absolute scientific standards from dawn to dusk. He strongly believes that subjective, perception-based living is the root cause of our problems at both a physical and mental level.

Dr. Kumar is also a practitioner and teacher of Kayachikitsa, which is an ancient science focused on the treatment of physical and mental illness. He is one of only three people in the world to have been trained and currently practice in this particular system.

Dr. Kumar leads intensive training in meditation (Raja yoga) theory and practice leading to profound insight and liberation. This is taught as per the Yoga Shastras and Yoga Sanhitha given by Yajnavalkya and later by Patanjali in the form of Yoga Darshana. Besides his online training courses that run continuously throughout the year, Dr. Kumar currently teaches two to three immersive residential retreats in India annually, holding at least one in the summer and one in the winter. These retreats are for periods of 10 or 15 days depending on the venue and related circumstances.

Dr. Kumar is a consultant trainer at Yogacampus in London. Having trained yoga teachers for the last four decades, drawing on his experiential wisdom and knowledge developed from living and learning in many different ashrams, ancient traditions and from many different teachers, he teaches yoga for mind and body with authority.

In 2017 and 2018 Dr. Kumar was invited by the United Nations, International Labour Organization in Geneva, to lead celebrations for International Yoga Day. Outside of reputed Indian institutions, he has also given lectures at Oxford Brookes University, Bath Royal Literary and Scientific Institution, Imperial College London, SOAS (School of Oceanic and Asian Studies, University of London) and Kyungpook National University School of Medicine in Daegu, Korea.

EDUCATION

Rather than belonging to any one school, Dr. Kumar’s teaching adheres to the genesis of yoga and its many components. Dr Kumar’s first teacher cautioned him to avoid being limited to the methodology of only one school, realising that each ashram preserves only a few limbs of yoga.

Dr. Kumar has followed this advice throughout his life, allowing him to keep an open mind, and to continuously learn and expand his knowledge, respecting all traditions.

As a child he began his formal studies at the Sivananda Ashram under Swamy Vishnudevananda. He was initiated by Swami Chinmayananda, Sachidananda & Omkarananda and later under Swami Rama and Satyananda Saraswathi.

Dr Kumar also learned asana practice with BKS Iyengar, Pattabhi Jois and Desikachar among other renowned teachers as well as unknown but equally accomplished Himalayan masters and monks. He learned powerful health rejuvenating pranayamas in Shakthi traditions of Tantra such as Prakrithika (external respiratory techniques), Vaikrithika (internal respiratory techniques) and Kevala (natural respiration).

Other schools attended by Dr. Kumar include Kaivalyadhama-Lonovala, Ananda Ashram, Himachal and Kriya Yoga traditions such as Babaji Kriya Yoga, Yogoda, Mangeshda. His theoretical studies of Hatha Yoga have explored Hatha Yoga Pradipika, Hatha Ratnavali, Gheranda Samhita, Siva Samhita, Hatha Yoga Rahasyam, Kurantaka Yoga and Kapala Kurantaka Yoga and he has translated many of these original texts for his training program.

Dr. Kumar has studied meditation in Hindu, Yoga, Mahayana, and Theravada Buddhist schools including monastic traditions in Thailand, Burma, Sri Lanka and Tibet. His mastery of meditation, honouring the lineage of Patanjali is central to his teaching.

He reads Sanskrit and Pali and has sourced and translated rare texts, exploring the relationship between Yoga, Buddhism and Vedanta. In gratitude for the benefits he has received Dr. Kumar has volunteered his time to teaching yoga and meditation for free to thousands of people around the world and to reviving the ancient system of yoga therapy, Kayachikitsa.
