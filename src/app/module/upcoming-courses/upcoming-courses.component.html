Course related information

Course Description

Course format

Classes with Dr. Kumar : Every Monday, Wednesday and Friday.

Class time : Streamed live starting at 5am Indian time.

Class duration: Minimum 3hrs.

No fixed duration, varys in length each day.

Videos will be made available post class on the course page. You can watch at your convenience anytime.

It is not compulsory to watch live, but it is recommended for those in time zones where it is possible.

It is strongly recommended that you watch the classes live or on the same day as broadcast but if this is not possible you can complete the missed portion of the class on non-class days.

A sincere student, who is willing to dedicate 4 to 5 hours every day so as to complete and assimilate the daily classes and practice sessions is suitable for this course.

Certification

200 Hours and 500 Hours Certification (Accredited with International Yoga Alliance)

Certification is optional and you are also welcomed to just attend the course for your own personal learning.

For those taking the certification, a Traditional Yoga 200 hr and 500 hr certificate will be issued for those who maintain the Code of Discipline and who complete all the required elements of the course including assignments, plus any additional tasks and homework given by Dr. Kumar in the classes, by the end of each course.

Traditional Yoga is a registered Yoga School with International Yoga Alliance (IYA). Therefore, the certificate issued to you at the end of each course can be used to register with IYA if you wish to apply for their accreditation.



Booking Information

Courses are offered for free as per ancient traditions of yoga. There is no fee associated with courses.

1.The aspirant is requested to fill all the fields of a course application by clicking on “Enroll Now”.
2.The aspirant understands that they are reading and agreeing to the Code of Discipline & Joining Agreement when submitting their application.
3.Please read the Code of Discipline, to understand about the Guru, the Tradition (sampradaya) and the Lineage (parampara).

Experience of Aspirants with past meditation practices (from other disciplines) is not considered as either eligibility or merit for approval. Rather, aspirants are mandated to read, understand and assimilate the Code of Discipline in detail.

Certification

Be ready to unlearn and relearn with an open mind. You will be observed during the first month on the following criteria in order to gain full approval. Full approval will allow you to continue on the online course which you have applied for after the first month:

1. Writing Gratitude Messages every week: the tradition of expressing Modana (gratitude) every moment by Anumodana (expressing joy by listening to Guru) only positivity by body, mind and speech, irrespective of anything and everything.

2. Response to the mentor or volunteer or counsellor and compliance with suggestions given by them and showing respect in gratitude to their service to the tradition and yoga as a pure charity.

3. Keeping up with the course on a day-to-day basis.

4. Practising the given techniques and submitting their practice demos (through videos) for correction and compliance to correction.

5. Compliance to suggested modifications with respect to diet, nutritional advice, sleep and important to day- to-day aspects of life.

6. Any personalities with criticism, blame, being negative in approach and attitude, egoistic behaviour, stubbornness, insensitivity, lies, lethargy and laziness in any way is discouraged and will lead to removal from the course without any notice.
