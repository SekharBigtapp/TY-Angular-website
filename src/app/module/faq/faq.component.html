Section 1: Things to know prior to applying for Traditional Yoga (T.Y.) courses

The eternal science of Yoga has survived through the ages as a central element of culture. Gurus taught students the scientific ways of living like Yogis from birth to death and beyond. It was never something that was sold for profit but a way of living taught to benefit all.


Traditional Yoga is simply continuing this tradition of teaching authentic ways of living like a Yogi in a material world where Yoga has become commercialized and fragmented with mostly asana-oriented programs.

Yoga Healing Foundation is a registered charity with a voluntary donation system that is able to offer free online courses under the name Traditional Yoga.

T.Y. Teacher Training Courses run continuously throughout the year with 3 courses on an average per year.

The online application form for new courses is made available on our website when enrollments are open. You can stay updated on new course information by subscribing to our website and following us on our social media channels for updates. You can sign up by submitting the online application form.

Section 4 - About Traditional Yoga (T.Y.) donations

Donations are on a voluntary basis, but it is highly encouraged in the Tradition to donate to develop the quality of generosity and also so that more and more people can benefit from the program.

Gurudakshina is not course fees. In traditional ashrams, Gurudakshina is given to the Guru at Graduation/ Samarohana to clear spiritual indebtedness to the Guru, the Teachings, and the Lineage of Yoga. This practice stems from ashram culture where all students ask at the end, what the Guru requires of them with a heart that is ready to give anything to the Guru for everything given freely with love, compassion, and generosity.

T.Y. has stipulated the minimum Gurudakshina amount you need to donate at INR 2,500 OR USD 35 OR EUR 25 at the end of each course to clear the spiritual indebtedness you accrue.

Students who truly cannot afford this amount due to any contingencies can express their inability to pay and seek assistance at that time.

Guruji - Dr. A.L.V. Kumar, believes in transparency when it comes to disclosing where the donation money comes from to run T.Y. freely. So the growing generosity of aspirants on the path of enlightenment are acknowledged to give them the blessings of the pool of consciousness.


Section 5: About Traditional Yoga (T.Y.) higher education programs

Traditional Yoga offers diplomas, bachelor’s degrees, and master’s degrees in the field of Yoga with University Grants Commission (U.G.C.) accreditations. Please note that U.G.C. degrees are ONLY applicable to Indians across the world.

The U.G.C. is the apex body in India that approves and recognizes valid and valuable degree programs offered by universities, colleges, and institutions. All Traditional Yoga university programs are recognized by the U.G.C.

Degrees/ Diplomas in below streams:

  • Yoga Clinical
  • Naturopathy Clinical
  • Acupuncture and E.M.
  • Nutrition and Diet

Eligibility: Intermediate/ 10+2

  • U.G. Diploma (Yoga & Naturopathy – 1 year)
  • B.Y.N. (3 years)
  • B.N.Y.S. (5 & 6 years)

Eligibility: Graduation

  • Postgraduate Diploma (Yoga & Naturopathy – 1 year)
  • M.A. (Yoga & Naturopathy – 2 years)

* Subject to paying university fees by students.

Indian students living abroad with an Indian passport can apply for the university programs offered by T.Y.

Students opting for degrees in different streams have to attend different classes which will be conducted by T.Y. in addition to the regular yoga classes.

Students are subject to paying university fees (registrations fees, examination fees, etc.) for a particular degree program they are following. For more information on specific courses, fill the ‘Get in Touch’ form.
